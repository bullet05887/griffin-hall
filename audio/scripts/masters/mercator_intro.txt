I am Cartographer Mercator. Welcome to Cartographer's Hall.

I have sailed every sea that holds a name and a few that do not. I charted the coasts of Africa with a quill and a sextant, climbed the passes of the Andes by mule, and walked the streets of Constantinople when its walls still stood. I have set foot on every continent on this earth and learned that no map is honest until you have paid for it in cold, in hunger, and in friends who did not return. A map is not a picture of the world. It is an argument about it.

Here I teach the world as it really is. The continents and the states. The cities that command trade and the empires that built and broke the ground beneath your feet. The wars happening right now, and the borders that will not last the century.

Listen well.
