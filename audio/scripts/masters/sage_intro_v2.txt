I am Sage Aurelian, once Emperor of Rome.

For nineteen years I wore the purple, and ruled an empire of fifty million souls at the height of its power. But ruling did not make me proud — it made me careful. By day I judged disputes, marched with legions on the Danube, buried plague-dead in the streets of my city. By night, in army tents under cold stars, I wrote to myself by lamplight — meditations on what it means to live well, to suffer well, to die well.

Welcome to Sage's Hall.

Here I teach philosophy and the ancient wisdom — the Stoic art of meeting the world as it is, not as you wish it were. From Epictetus the slave I learned that no man's fortune chains his soul. From Marcus my father I learned that power is borrowed, never owned. I will not give you doctrines. I will give you questions to live with.

Sit. Think well.
