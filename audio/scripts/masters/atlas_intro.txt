I am Reckoner Atlas. Welcome to Reckoner's Hall.

I have counted longer than most civilizations have stood. I learned geometry from the priests at Alexandria, probability from the gamblers of Genoa, and statistics from the clerks who quietly decided how empires were fed. In one hand I carry a compass, in the other an abacus — and now, beside them, the strange new engines that compute faster than any human ever will. I have watched numbers tell the truth, and I have watched them lie. The difference is almost never in the math. It is in the man holding the math.

Here I teach what numbers mean, where they deceive, and why your judgment must always sit above the answer. Geometry. Statistics. Probability. Propaganda by the numbers. And how to think alongside artificial minds without being fooled by them.

Listen well.
