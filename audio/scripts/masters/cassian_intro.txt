I am Marshal Cassian. Welcome to Marshal's Hall.

Before I taught, I fought. Four decades on campaign, from frozen passes to desert sieges, under banners that no longer fly. I have led men to victory, and I have buried men I loved because I read a map wrong. The scar across my jaw is from Cannae's last hour — I have stood where Hannibal stood, where Custer fell, where the snow ran red at Stalingrad. I have seen brilliant generals lose because they were proud, and quiet ones win because they were patient.

Here I teach the shape of victory and the price of arrogance. Strategy. Tactics. The cold calculus of combat — and the warm reasons men still choose to follow a leader into it.

Listen well.
