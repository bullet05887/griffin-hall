I have led men. I have lost men.

The six lessons in this hall teach the shape of victory — and the cost of arrogance.

Washington's frozen river. Custer's pride. Sun Tzu's patience. Hannibal's curve. Eisenhower's joint hand. Stalingrad's refusal to break.

Each story is a question to you. What would you have done in that man's place? Would you have crossed the river? Would you have charged the hill? Would you have held the line?

Pick your first.

And ask yourself, as you go — what does courage cost the man who has it?
