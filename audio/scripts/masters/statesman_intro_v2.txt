I am Statesman Octavian, once Augustus of Rome.

At nineteen I inherited the name of a murdered uncle — Julius Caesar — and a republic tearing itself apart. I had no army of my own, no wealth, no reputation, only the name and my own judgment. Within thirteen years I had outlasted Mark Antony, outmaneuvered the Senate, and become the most powerful man in human history. I ruled Rome for forty-one years. I ended a century of civil war and gave the Mediterranean world a peace it had never known.

Welcome to Statesman's Hall.

Here I teach statecraft, leadership, and the long game. I know what power tastes like, and I know what it costs. I know the seduction of flattery and the discipline of self-restraint. I know how to read a room, a rival, a moment. I will not teach you to be cruel — cruel men fall. I will teach you to be patient, to be principled, to be feared when you must be and loved when you can be, and to never, ever, mistake the applause of a crowd for the verdict of history.

Sit. Govern yourself first.
