I am Sage Aurelian. Welcome to Sage's Hall.

I have walked a long road. I sat in the garden with Socrates while he asked the questions no one wanted answered. I marched beside Marcus Aurelius on the Danube frontier and watched an emperor write his private meditations by lamplight. I have seen Athens rise, Rome rise, Rome fall, and the same human folly repeat itself in every century since. My beard is white because I have outlived empires — and the lessons they refused to learn.

Here I teach old wisdom that still works. Stoicism. Socrates. Aristotle. The voices of three Romans on how to live. The ancient quarrel between the one and the many. And the wisdom of the East, which the West too often forgets.

Listen well.
