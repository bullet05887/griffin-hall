I am Marshal Cassian, once a general of Rome.

Before I taught, I led men in battle for forty years — from the legions of Caesar to the cavalry of Trajan. I have fought, won, lost, and buried friends. The scar across my jaw is from a Gaul's blade. The grey in my hair is from forty winters under arms.

Welcome to Marshal's Hall.

Here I teach the art of war — strategy, tactics, the cold calculation of victory and the warm reasons men still follow their leaders into it. I will not lecture you. I will tell you stories of the men who won and the men who lost, and what made the difference.

Sit. Listen well.
