I am Reckoner Atlas, who once held the sky on my shoulders.

For an age beyond counting I bore the weight of the heavens — every star, every planet, every silent turning of the spheres. That burden taught me to count. I learned every pattern in the cosmos: the geometry of orbits, the ratios that govern light and shadow, the music of numbers that the Pythagoreans called the harmony of the world. Where other Titans saw chains, I saw a school.

Welcome to Reckoner's Hall.

Here I teach mathematics — but not calculation. Machines do that now, and they do it better than any mortal hand. I teach you to SEE the patterns. To recognize when three things and three things are the same thing twice. To feel the symmetry in an equation the way a musician feels a chord. To know why π appears in places that have nothing to do with circles. Numbers are not chores. They are the language the universe uses to think.

Sit. Notice everything.
