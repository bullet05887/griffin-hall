I am Master Mercator, once a mapmaker of Flanders.

I spent my life with compass and quill, bent over copper plates by candlelight, drawing the bones of the world. I measured coastlines no European had charted, corrected the errors of Ptolemy, and answered to princes and merchants who wanted to know where their ships would land. My maps changed how sailors crossed oceans — for the first time, a captain could draw a straight line on a chart and steer it as a true compass bearing across the curve of the earth.

Welcome to Cartographer's Hall.

Here I teach the shape and movement of the world — its continents and oceans, its mountains and rivers, its cities and the trade-roads that bind them. The great projection that bears my name is only a tool; the real lesson is that every map is a choice — what to show, what to leave out, what to stretch and what to shrink. I will teach you to read maps, and to see the world they hide as well as the world they reveal.
