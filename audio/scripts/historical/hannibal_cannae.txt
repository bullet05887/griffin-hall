I am Hannibal Barca. I crossed the Alps with elephants and made Rome shake for sixteen years. Listen, and I will tell you how it was. I was a Carthaginian general. Carthage was a city in North Africa at war with Rome. To attack Rome, I marched my army, including thirty-seven war elephants, across the snowy Alps into Italy. Most generals would have called that impossible. I did not. In 216 BC, I met a Roman army at a place called Cannae. The Romans had about eighty-six thousand men. I had about fifty thousand. The Romans figured they would just smash forward with their bigger force and win the way they usually won. I put my weakest troops in the center, in a curved line that bulged toward the Romans. I put my best troops on the wings — the sides. When the Romans pushed forward, my weak center slowly pulled back. The Roman army poured into the middle like water into a bowl. Then my wings swung around behind them. The Romans were surrounded on all four sides. By sundown, they had lost about fifty thousand soldiers. Cannae is still studied as the perfect double envelopment.
